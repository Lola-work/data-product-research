The question everyone asks

Is it still a chatbot on a database?

No. That was three years ago. The industry moved through two phases and is now in a third — and saying "we added AI chat" in 2026 marks you as two cycles behind.

The threat

ChatGPT can already do the search part

Deep-research modes in ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini and Perplexity do multi-step literature synthesis with citations, for free or near it. On open-access content, they've largely commoditized "find and summarize."

Then there's the flaw.

Fabricated citations in published biomedical papers, per 10,000

The Lancet, May 2026 — an audit of 2.5m papers and 125.6m references. Review articles fabricate 57% more than average.

Two measured values, three years apart. The dashed line shows direction, not data — the paper reports that the rise begins mid-2024, but the shape between the endpoints is not published.

It helps. It doesn't fix it. Even with retrieval grounding, 3–13% of generated URLs are still fabricated. And raw GPT-4 recall on literature retrieval measured 0.073 against 0.782 for a structured tool — it isn't just making things up, it's also missing almost everything.

This is the incumbents' entire counter-argument, and it's the strongest card they hold.

The trap

They're selling rope to the hangman

Publishers found a lucrative new revenue line: licensing their archives to AI labs. It's real money, and it may be the most dangerous thing they're doing.

The cash is non-recurring. The capability transfer is permanent. Every archive licensed for training makes the next general-purpose model a little better at exactly the thing you charge for.

The escape: license for inference with attribution and metering, not for training. Sell access to the living, structured, traceable asset — not a frozen copy of it.

The answer

What a data product has to be now

Seven things. The first three are what a general model can't copy. The rest are what turns that into a business.

Provenance, to the paragraph. Contradictions surfaced, confidence calibrated, trail logged. This is the differentiator, not a feature.
Structure, not text. Normalized to real ontologies — MeSH, SNOMED, Emtree. Norstella tags to 55 of them; Causaly's graph holds 500m+ facts. Flat PDFs are a commodity.
The stuff Google can't see. Conference abstracts, patents, trial registries, regulatory filings, the customer's own internal documents.
Be readable by other people's agents. MCP endpoints and APIs. Owning the context layer may matter more than owning the interface.
Survive an audit. Zero-retention terms, cleared rights, reproducible logs. The FDA's credibility framework makes this the price of entry, not a nice-to-have.
Publish your error rate. Everyone quotes their own accuracy numbers today. That era ends the moment a buyer asks for an independent benchmark.
Live inside the workflow. UpToDate went into the EHR, not the browser. The destination website is a declining asset — and agents don't have seats, which breaks per-seat pricing.

The one-sentence version

AI ate the search box. What it can't eat is structured, rights-cleared, traceable data delivered into a regulated workflow — so that's the only place left to build.
