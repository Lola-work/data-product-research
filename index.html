Method

How these are made

The reports argue that provenance is the moat. It would be embarrassing not to practise it.

Filings over commentary. Company results and regulator documents first. Vendor press releases are treated as claims, not facts. Analyst market-sizing is treated with suspicion.
Every report ships a sources file. Claim by claim, with a confidence rating and the date it was verified. Linked from the report itself.
Estimated data is labelled on the chart. Not in a footnote. A visual argument shouldn't smooth a curve the evidence doesn't support.
Gaps are stated. Each sources file ends with what couldn't be verified. If the thesis rests on something soft, that's the reader's business.
Interpretation is flagged as mine. The financials belong to the companies. The argument doesn't. Where I'm reading into the numbers, the sources file says so.
Nothing gets quietly edited. Corrections are commits. The history is public — including the bits I got wrong.

Found an error? Open an issue. Corrections get credited.
